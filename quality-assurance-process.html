    Quality Assurance Process at Techno Fab

    At Techno Fab, quality is at the core of everything we do. Our rigorous Quality Assurance (QA) process ensures that every product and service meets the highest industry standards and exceeds customer expectations. This systematic approach spans all stages of production, from initial planning to final delivery.

    Quality Assurance Objectives

    • Ensure consistent product quality and reliability.
    • Comply with international standards and customer-specific requirements.
    • Minimize defects, rework, and delays.
    • Foster a culture of continuous improvement.
    • Enhance customer satisfaction and build long-term trust.

    QA Process Flow

    1. Pre-Production Stage

    • Requirement Analysis: Review customer specifications and standards, develop quality plans, and identify critical control points.
    • Supplier Evaluation and Material Inspection: Conduct supplier audits, verify certifications, and inspect incoming materials.
    • Process Planning: Define QA checkpoints, create process flow charts, and assign roles.

    2. Production Stage

    • In-Process Inspection: Monitor key production stages, check dimensions, weld quality, and surface finishes, and use NDT methods like ultrasonic and radiography.
    • Process Control: Maintain process parameters, traceability, and use quality control tools such as SPC.
    • In-Process Documentation: Record inspection results and address non-conformities with root cause analysis (RCA) and corrective action reports (CARs).

    3. Post-Production Stage

    • Final Inspection and Testing: Conduct dimensional, mechanical, and environmental tests, along with load and performance checks.
    • Quality Audit and Verification: Perform internal audits and verify corrective actions
    • Document Review and Certification: Approve inspection reports and issue certificates of compliance (CoC).

    Quality Control Tools and Techniques

    • Non-Destructive Testing (NDT): Ultrasonic, Radiography, Magnetic Particle, and Dye Penetrant testing.
    • Inspection Checklists: Customized for each production stage.
    • Control Charts: Track process variation.
    • Root Cause Analysis (RCA): Identify and eliminate defect causes.
    • Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA): Address deficiencies effectively.

    Roles and Responsibilities

    • QA Manager: Oversees QA implementation, audits, and improvements.
    • Quality Inspectors: Conduct in-process and final inspections.
    • Production Team: Ensure compliance with QA procedures.
    • Suppliers: Deliver defect-free materials meeting quality standards.

    Continuous Improvement and Training

    • Regular training sessions on QA standards and techniques.
    • Promote Kaizen and Lean methodologies for continuous improvement.
    • Encourage employee feedback for process enhancements.

    Quality Standards and Certifications

    • Adhere to ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System (QMS).
    • Comply with industry-specific standards like ASME and AWS D1.1.
    • Achieve customer-specific certifications for specialized projects.

    Documentation and Records

    • Quality Manual
    • Inspection Test Plans (ITPs)
    • Quality Control Plans (QCPs)
    • Supplier Audit Reports
    • Material Test Certificates (MTCs)
    • Inspection Reports and Non-Conformance Reports (NCRs)
    • Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) records

    Customer Feedback and Complaint Handling

    • Establish feedback mechanisms to address quality issues.
    • Investigate and resolve complaints promptly with corrective actions.
    • Use feedback to refine QA processes and prevent future issues.

    Conclusion

    At Techno Fab, we believe "Quality is not an act; it is a habit." Our QA process is designed to consistently deliver products that exceed customer expectations. By adhering to international standards and fostering a culture of continuous improvement, we ensure operational excellence and long-term customer satisfaction
