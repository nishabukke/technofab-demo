    What Are Pre-Engineered Buildings?

    Pre-Engineered Buildings (PEBs) are innovative steel structures manufactured at a factory and assembled on-site. Known for their affordability, durability, and flexibility, PEBs are widely used in industrial, commercial, and institutional construction projects.

    The Concept: Revolutionizing Steel Construction

    PEBs are a game-changer in the construction industry, offering efficiency, speed, and cost-effectiveness. Here's what makes them unique:

    Material Optimization

    PEBs are designed using advanced engineering techniques to minimize steel usage without compromising strength. Components are tailored with varying thicknesses based on load requirements, ensuring lightweight yet robust structures.

    Pre-Fabrication

    All components are precision-engineered in a controlled factory environment. This eliminates the variability of traditional construction methods and guarantees superior quality and accuracy.

    Quick Assembly

    PEBs arrive at the site ready for bolt-together assembly, significantly reducing construction time and labor costs.

    Since their inception in the 1960s, PEBs have transformed construction by offering:

  • Cost Savings: Optimized material usage and efficient assembly reduce costs.
  • Faster Completion: Pre-fabricated parts enable quicker project turnaround.
  • Long-Lasting Durability: Steel construction ensures resilience and structural integrity.

    • Key Architectural Features Include:

  • Building Width: Distance between the outer eave struts of sidewalls.
  • Building Length: Distance between the outer flanges of end wall columns.
  • End Bay Length: Distance from the outer flange of end wall columns to the first interior frame.
  • Interior Bay Length: Spacing between adjacent interior main frames, ranging from 6m to 15m.
  • Building Height: Eave height up to 30m, measured from the base plate to the top of the eave strut.
  • Roof Slope: Angle of the roof, typically at 1:10 for optimal drainage and aesthetics.
